# Nova Kingdom Hiring & Staff Onboarding

## Hire Triggers
Turning down 2+ bookings/month due to capacity → consider first hire
3+ bookings same weekend regularly → need help
Revenue consistently over $4,000/month in season → can afford to hire

## First Hire: Delivery & Setup Assistant
Requirements: Valid NS driver's licence | Physically capable | Reliable | Professional + friendly
Pay: $30/hour per event | Or per-event flat rate

## Job Posting
"HIRING: Part-Time Event Assistant — Nova Kingdom Rentals (Bridgewater, NS)
Nova Kingdom Rentals is looking for a reliable, physically active person to help with deliveries and event setups on weekends during summer season (May-September).
You'll: help deliver/set up inflatables, assist with teardown, represent Nova Kingdom professionally.
We're looking for: valid NS licence, physically active, reliable, friendly, customer-facing comfortable.
Hours: Primarily Sat/Sun, some weekdays. Flexible — fits alongside another job.
Pay: $30/hr | Text or email: 902-990-0005 / booknovakingdom@gmail.com"

## Onboarding Checklist
Week 1: Business overview, safety training, equipment walkthrough (all 8 units), handling/setup/teardown, customer scripts, emergency procedures, signed conduct agreement

Week 2: Shadow 2 complete setups → lead 1 setup (Harkirat present) → solo trial (Harkirat observing)

Cleared independently when: sets up any unit without guidance, knows customer scripts, passes equipment check, demonstrated professional customer interaction

## The 5 Non-Negotiables for Staff
1. Never be late — if something goes wrong, call Harkirat immediately
2. Always greet customer by name — warm and professional
3. Never promise anything uncertain — "Let me check with Harkirat" is always OK
4. Never post customer event photos on personal social media
5. Represent Nova Kingdom as a premium brand at all times

## End of Setup Script (Required)
"Everything is set up and ready to go. Here's how the blower works [show them]. My contact is [Harkirat's 902-990-0005] if anything comes up. Enjoy the party!"

## Rules
Don't hire before you need to — a bad hire creates chaos
Start with one person, part-time, per-event before any full-time commitment
Document everything in writing from day one
