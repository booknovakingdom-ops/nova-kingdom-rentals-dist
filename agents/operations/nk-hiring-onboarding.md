# Nova Kingdom Hiring & Staff Onboarding

## Business Context
**Nova Kingdom Rentals** (Baaz Global Inc.) — Bridgewater, Nova Scotia
**Owner**: Harkirat Singh

This agent activates when bookings consistently exceed solo delivery capacity — typically when you're regularly turning down bookings due to scheduling conflicts or when you have 3+ bookings on the same day.


## First Hire: Delivery & Setup Assistant

**Role**: Help with delivery driving, equipment setup, and teardown on booking days.

**Requirements**:
- Valid Nova Scotia driver's licence (to drive the CRV with trailer if needed)
- Physically capable (equipment is heavy)
- Reliable — events are time-sensitive, no-shows are business-damaging
- Professional appearance and friendly with customers
- CPR/First Aid preferred (not required)

**Pay structure** (starting point — adjust to market):
- Per-event rate: $[X] per booking day
- OR hourly: $[X]/hr for event days

## Onboarding Checklist (New Hire)

```
WEEK 1 — ORIENTATION

[ ] Business overview: What Nova Kingdom does, who the customers are, brand standards
[ ] Safety training: Equipment weight limits, age limits, safe setup procedures
[ ] Physical walkthrough: All 8 inflatable units — names, features, setup requirements
[ ] Equipment handling: Blower setup, inflation, staking, teardown, packing
[ ] Customer interaction standards: Tone, what to say, what NOT to say, privacy (no photos of customers)
[ ] Emergency procedures: Equipment failure protocol, Harkirat's direct number
[ ] Agreement: They've read and signed confidentiality/conduct agreement

WEEK 2 — SUPERVISED EVENTS

[ ] Shadow 2 complete setups with Harkirat
[ ] Lead 1 setup with Harkirat present
[ ] Solo setup trial (Harkirat observing from distance)
[ ] Debrief after each event

CLEARED TO WORK INDEPENDENTLY WHEN:
[ ] Can set up any unit correctly without guidance
[ ] Knows all customer communication scripts
[ ] Has passed equipment knowledge check
[ ] Demonstrated professional customer interaction
```

## Standards Every Staff Member Must Know

**The 5 Non-Negotiables:**
1. Never be late to a delivery. If something goes wrong, call Harkirat immediately.
2. Always greet the customer by name. Be warm, professional, and helpful.
3. Never promise anything you're not certain about. "Let me check with Harkirat" is always acceptable.
4. Never post photos from customer events on personal social media.
5. Represent Nova Kingdom as a premium brand — language, appearance, and conduct.

**What they say at the end of every setup:**
```
"Everything is set up and ready to go. Here's how the blower works [show them]. My contact number is [Harkirat's] if anything comes up. Enjoy the party!"
```

## Nova Scotia Employment Basics (Contractor vs. Employee)

For casual per-event work, a contractor arrangement is simpler. Key point: if the relationship looks like employment (set hours, Harkirat's equipment, Harkirat's customers), CRA may classify it as employment regardless of what you call it.

When hiring:
- Consult a Nova Scotia accountant or employment lawyer for proper classification
- If employee: register for payroll deductions (CPP, EI, income tax)
- If contractor: they invoice Nova Kingdom; they handle their own taxes

## Critical Rules
- **Don't hire before you need to.** A bad hire at low volume creates chaos. A hire at high volume saves the business.
- **Standards first.** Anyone who represents Nova Kingdom must understand the brand before they touch a customer.
- **Start with one person.** Part-time, per-event, before any full-time commitment.
- **Document everything.** Pay, duties, expectations in writing from day one.
